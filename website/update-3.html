Summary

Third update is focused on PS2-era visual atmosphere: palette quantization, dither patterning, depth haze, and vertex jitter feel.

The goal is not photorealism. The goal is character: imperfect geometry response, compressed color ramps, and the iconic unstable edge feel that made many early-2000s games visually memorable.

Planned Additions

  • Toggleable PS2 mode in settings
  • Color banding and ordered dither
  • Vertex snap / wobble controls
  • Retro fog tuning presets

Status

This release page is marked as future and will be updated as visual tuning progresses.

Current direction is to keep the effect configurable so users can move between subtle retro treatment and aggressive PS2-like stylization depending on scene type.

Technical Direction

Vertex Stage

Apply controlled position snapping and mild jitter in clip-space to emulate unstable vertex precision.

Fragment Stage

Quantize final color output and add ordered dither to preserve readability while keeping retro texture.

Atmosphere

Use stronger depth haze and reduced color contrast to recreate long-distance PS2 mood.

Release Criteria

Update 03 will be considered complete when the effect is visually strong, stable during camera movement, and fully controllable from the in-engine settings without introducing disruptive artifacts.

The final delivery will include calibrated defaults, practical presets, and updated screenshots captured from real runtime scenes.
